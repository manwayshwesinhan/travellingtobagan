Bagan Travel Tips

Geography

Located in central Myanmar, Bagan is 400 miles north of the capital Yangon

Language

Aside from the Myanmar language, English is spoken at hotels and other tourist spots.

Time Difference

+06:50 GMT

$Currency$

Local currency is the Kyat. United States Dollars (US$) and Foreign Exchange Certificates (FEC) are accepted at hotels.

Donation can be made at every pagodas and temples. There is an office where people can donate money.

Weather

Bagan enjoys a dry climate throughout most of the year. Average temperature during the summer months (April to September) is around 32 degrees, and around 28 degrees the rest of the year.

Transportation..?

Air - The airport in Bagan is Nyaung U airport, located in Nyaung U region. There are daily flights from Yangon to Bagan. The carrier flights are provided by Yangon Airways, Air Mandalay, Myanma Airways and Air Bagan. The flights take about an hour and ten minutes from Yangon.

Train - from Yangon and Mandalay stop at Tharzi junction. This station is where it is accessible to Bagan by a 3 hour drive.

Express - There are regular coaches from Yangon to Bagan and Mandalay to Bagan. It will take about 10 hours from Yangon to Bagan.

- Before You Go -

What to wear

Bagan has a hot and humid weather. Light clothing will be appropriate for visitors. Shorts & short skirts are not appropriate especially when visiting any religious site.Sensible walking shoes, Hat, caps are also needed if you are travelling all day in Bagan.

Camera

Video cameras and photo cameras are allowed to be taken onto the temples and pagodas. But there may be some charges for carrying theses.

Water

Visitors are advised to drink only bottled or boiled water. Bottled drinking water is available in the guest rooms, compliments of Thiripyitsaya.
